Some of the leading figures of pneumonia

Pneumonia is the leading infectious killer of children under five years of age.

  • In 2015 alone, more than 900,000 children died from this preventable and treatable illness, accounting for 16% of under-five child mortality worldwide, 3% of which are newborns.
  • In 2008, there were an estimated 203,000 deaths due to Haemophilus influenzae type b (Hib) and 541,000 deaths due to Streptococcus pneumoniae (pneumococcus) in children under five.2
  • Scaling up coverage of vaccines against Hib and pneumococcus in the world’s 73 poorest countries (2011-2020) would avert $51 billion dollars in treatment costs and productivity losses.
  • This increase in vaccine coverage would also save 2.9 million lives and prevent 52 million cases of illness.
  • Pneumonia is the world’s leading cause of death among children under 5 years of age, accounting for 16% of all deaths of children under 5 years old killing approximately 2,400 children a day in 2015
  • . In the US, pneumonia is less often fatal for children, but it is still a big problem. Pneumonia is the #1 most common reason for US children to be hospitalized.
  • Pneumonia accounts for 15% of all deaths of children under 5 years old, killing 808 694 children in 2017.
